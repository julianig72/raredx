5. Ataxia-Oculomotor Apraxia-4 or Neurodegeneration with DNA Repair Deficiency (PNKP-related) · low
Gen(es): PNKP  ·  Herencia: Autosomal recessive  ·  Variante(s): 19:50364954 T>C (splice_acceptor_variant)
PNKP encodes a DNA repair enzyme and is associated with ataxia-oculomotor apraxia and neurodegeneration; seizures are not a primary feature. The heterozygous splice acceptor variant is pathogenic in ClinVar (though with 0 stars, indicating low confidence), and gnomAD AF of 3.6e-06 is appropriately rare. However, critical concerns undermine support: (1) PNKP disease requires biallelic mutations (autosomal recessive inheritance), and only a single heterozygous variant is reported—the potential second hit is unknown or absent; (2) the primary phenotype (ataxia, oculomotor apraxia, cancer predisposition, neurodegeneration) does not match infantile-onset seizures and epileptic spasms; and (3) infantile presentation with seizures as the leading sign is incompatible with typical PNKP disease progression. This variant is likely benign or represents incomplete disease segregation.
Siguiente paso: If PNKP is pursued, perform whole-gene sequencing to identify a potential second allele (required for recessive disease confirmation); assess for ataxia and oculomotor apraxia; if absent and no second mutation identified, exclude PNKP as the primary diagnosis.
